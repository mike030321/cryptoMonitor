I want your architectural review before implementation.

Based on the recent training evidence, the best design may not be “make every timeframe trade.” The evidence suggests 1h / 2h / 6h / 1d are not directionally predictive enough under the current model design, even after full-quality retraining. So I want you to evaluate and either agree or disagree with the following design proposal.

## Proposed design

Separate each timeframe by the role it has actually earned from evidence.

### Trade-authority timeframes

Only timeframes with promoted slices may open trades.

Current likely candidates:

* `1m` — trade candidate, but cost-sensitive
* `5m` — future trade candidate if data coverage and gates pass

### Governance-only timeframes

Higher timeframes should not open trades under current evidence.

Current proposed classification:

* `1h` — governance/filter only
* `2h` — governance/filter only
* `6h` — governance/filter only
* `1d` — macro/regime context only

These higher timeframes may still be useful for:

* regime detection
* risk-on / risk-off context
* volatility state
* suppression signals
* sizing pressure
* meta-brain context

But they should not directly open trades unless future evidence proves they can clear the gate honestly.

## Architecture principle

Do not force every slice to become a trade predictor.

Use each horizon for what it can prove:

* fast horizons = tactical execution
* slow horizons = context/governance/risk filtering

### 6. Hard rules

* Do not weaken gates.
* Do not turn quant on globally.
* Do not allow higher timeframes to trade just because a model exists.
* Do not hide failed timeframes.
* Do not call a governance-only timeframe “live trading.”
* Do not add new architecture unless this role split is clearly justified by evidence.
* Do not treat this as a UI-only change; it must protect runtime behavior too if implemented.
