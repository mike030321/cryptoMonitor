## Task direction

Use:

**Quintile-Based / Sparse Opportunity Labels**

## Goal

Replace the current UP/DOWN/STABLE directional target with a label design that tries to identify rare tradeable opportunities after costs.

The current model asks:

“Will price go up/down/stable?”

The new research should ask:

“Is this candle in the top-quality subset of future post-cost opportunity?”

## Requirements

Compare the current 3-class label against at least one new label family:

### Candidate A — quintile future-return labels

Bucket future post-cost returns into quintiles, such as:

* Q5 = strongest long opportunity
* Q1 = strongest short opportunity
* Q2–Q4 = no-trade / weak opportunity

### Candidate B — sparse trade/no-trade labels

Label only the strongest future opportunities as trade candidates:

* long_candidate
* short_candidate
* no_trade

### Candidate C — post-cost opportunity labels

Use fees/slippage/friction in the label:

* trade only if future move exceeds round-trip cost + safety margin

## Evaluation

Do not judge success by directional accuracy alone.

Evaluate:

* post-fee net PnL
* trade count
* average return per trade
* max drawdown
* precision of selected trades
* directional_call_share
* abstain/no-trade rate
* comparison to baseline
* calibration of trade probability
